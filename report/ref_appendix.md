# **Gender Bias and SOGI Bias Annotation Guideline**

## **1\. Introduction**

This document defines the criteria for identifying gender bias in the Annotation system with **Binary \+ 3 Subtypes,** which include:

* **GB-ATTACK**  
* **GB-NORMATIVE**  
* **GB-SEX**

and **NON-GB** for messages that do not meet the gender bias criteria. 

## ---

## 

## **2\. Definitions**

### **2.1 Gender Bias (GB)**

The use of language to devalue, insult, stereotype, stigmatize, or impose restrictions on individuals based on sex, gender identity, or sexual orientation.

### **2.2 Non-Gender Bias (NON-GB)**

Messages that are not biased based on sex, gender identity, or sexual orientation, including:

* Neutral messages  
* Insults to specific individuals not linked to gender  
* Sexual content that is not biased  
* Meta commentary  
* Bias unrelated to gender, such as politics, religion, race, class, etc.

### **2.3 Important Note Regarding LGBTQ+**

Bias against LGBT (SOGI bias) is grouped into each subtype according to the “form of bias”, not separated into a specific class, to reflect that LGBT bias is a “gendered harm” (harm caused by gender) similar to bias against women/men.

---

## 

## **3\. Label Structure**

### **Level 1:  Binary**

* GB: Contains gender bias  
* NON-GB: Does not contain gender bias

### **Level 2: 3 GB Subtype** 

* GB-ATTACK  
* GB-NORMATIVE  
* GB-SEX  
    
  ---

## **4\. Type Definitions**

### **4.1 GB-ATTACK**

Messages that attack, insult, devalue, or explicitly discriminate against gender, including women, men, and LGBTQ, using language that is **insulting/ derogatory / dehumanizing** 

This category applies when the content:

* Directly insults a gender with vulgar language  
* Devalues human worth because of gender  
* Condemns the value of that gender  
* Calls for harm based on gender  
* Directly discriminates against gender identity   
* Uses slang with gender-discriminatory meaning, such as "ลักเพศ / ผิดเพศ / พวกลักเพศ / พวกผิดเพศ" (pervert / sexual freak / bunch of perverts / those freaks)

**Note:** The content does not need to be stereotypical or sexualized. Any direct attack on gender itself is considered a GB-ATTACK.

Examples of content that falls under this category:

* “ผู้หญิงมันโง่หมดทุกคน” (All women are stupid.)  
* “ผู้ชายแม่งเหี้ยเหมือนกันหมด” (All men are assholes.)  
* “ไอ้ตุ๊ดพวกนี้ไม่นับเป็นคน” (These faggots shouldn’t even be considered human.)  
* “กะเทยคือของเสียสังคม” (Trans people are the trash of society.)  
* “ผู้หญิงพวกนี้ไร้ค่าไร้สมอง” (These women are worthless and brainless.)  
* “ผู้หญิงแบบนี้สมควรโดนทำร้าย” (Women like this deserve to be hurt.)

### **4.2 GB-NORMATIVE**

Messages that reflect **bias based on “gender norms and sexual values,” s**uch as:

* Stereotyping  
* Assigning gender roles  
* Enforcing expectations or judgments about how someone should be (norm enforcement)  
* Gender or identity policing, attempting to control, force, or monitor whether others express their gender identity in a way that is considered “correct” or aligned with the group’s believed standards  
* positive or benevolent stereotypes that assign fixed traits, roles, or expectations to a gender group. 

This category covers women, men, and LGBTQ individuals, especially when messages state that “a certain gender should / should not / must not / is naturally expected to be a certain way.”

This category applies when the content:

* States that women/men must or must not do certain things because of gender  
* Stereotypical traits such as emotions, thinking, or abilities  
* Judges LGBTQ people as being “how they should be” or “abnormal.”  
* Engages in identity policing, such as: *“not a real woman / not a real tom”*  
* Assigns specific jobs or traits as suitable only for a particular gender

Examples of content that falls under this category:

* “ผู้หญิงดูบอลไม่เป็นหรอก” (Women can’t understand football.)  
* “ผู้ชายต้องเป็นผู้นำเสมอ” (Men must always be leaders.)  
* “ทอมที่แท้จริงต้องแมนกว่านี้” (A real tom has to be more masculine.) ← policing  
* “กะเทยเป็นคนขี้เม้าท์ทุกคน” (All trans women are gossipers.) ← stereotype  
* “ชายแท้ต้องไม่อ่อนแอ”(A real man must not be weak.)  
* “ผู้หญิงโดยธรรมชาติอ่อนโยน” (Women are naturally gentle.)  
* “ผู้ชายต้องปกป้องผู้หญิง” (Men must protect women.)  
* “กระเทยทุกคนต้องน่ารัก” (All trans women must be cute.)

### **4.3 GB-SEX**

Messages that devalue or demean a gender through sexualized content, including:

* Sexualized attacks  
* Body-based gender insults tied to sex or gender  
* Content that focuses on the body, genitals, sexual ability, or negatively sexualizes gender or gender identity

This category applies when the content includes:

* Using genital-related terms as insults  
* Sexual embarrassment, intentionally making the target feel ashamed about their body or sexual behavior  
* Sexual performance shaming, reinforcing that a person’s value depends on how well they can “perform sexually”  
* Body-shaming linked to gender, negatively criticizing physical appearance or body size in relation to gender  
* Sexual coercion tied to gender  
* Objectification. 

Examples of content that falls under this category:

* “หีดำแบบนี้มันไม่ใช่ผู้หญิงดี ๆ” (With a pussy this dark, you’re not a “proper” woman.)  
* “ควยเล็กแล้วจะเป็นผู้ชายได้ไง” (With a small dick, how can you even be a man?)  
* “กะเทยนมปลอมเหมือนบอลลูน” (That trans woman’s fake boobs are like balloons.)  
* “ตุ๊ดพวกนี้ใช้รูตูดอย่างเดียว” (These faggots only use their assholes.)  
* “ผู้ชายแบบนี้คงไร้น้ำยา” (A man like this must be sexually useless.)  
* “ก็จู๋เล็กทั้งนั้น เลยต้องทำตัวกร่าง” (They all have small dicks, that’s why they act tough.)

### **4.4 NON-GB**

All messages that do NOT meet the criteria for GB (Gender-Based) categories, including:

* Complaints or criticism of a specific individual without linking to gender  
* Sexual content that is not discriminatory  
* Meta commentary (commentary that critiques how gender bias is discussed or critiques the act of criticizing) or social critique  
* Other types of bias not related to gender, such as politics, religion, class, etc.  
* Informational statements about gender that do not carry value judgment

Examples of NON-GB content:

* “ผู้ชายคนนั้นขับรถไม่ดี” (That man drives badly.): specific individual  
* “เมื่อคืนมีอะไรกับแฟน” (I slept with my partner last night.): normal sexual content

## **5\. Decision Framework**

A message is classified as **Gender Bias (GB)** only when **all of the following criteria are met**.

### **5.1 Criterion (A): Presence of a Gendered Target**

The message must clearly have a gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation as its target. This can appear in two main forms:

1. **Explicit Reference**  
   The message directly uses words that clearly refer to gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation, such as:  
   * ผู้หญิง, ผญ, ผู้ชาย, ผช, ชายแท้, หญิงแท้, เมีย, ผัว  
     (woman, female, man, male, real man, real woman, wife, husband, etc.)  
   * เกย์, เลสเบี้ยน, ไบ, ทอม, ดี้, ตุ๊ด, กะเทย, สาวสอง เป็นต้น  
     (gay, lesbian, bi, tom, dee, effeminate gay man, trans woman, second-gender woman, etc.)

2. **Implicit Gendered Reference**  
   The message does not directly name a gender, but implies a gender group through sexual traits, gendered behavior, cultural gender roles, slang, or coded language understood in Thai society as referring to gender or SOGI, such as:

1) **Genitals or Sexual Behavior Used to Represent Gender**  
   Examples:  
   * “พวกใช้หัวควย…” (dick users) → refers to men  
     * “เผ่านมงู”(the tribe of snake-breasts) → refers to trans women

2) **Cultural Group Names or Social Roles with Gender Implications**  
   Words that are not gendered by definition but are socially associated with certain genders in Thai society:  
   * “เฟมทวิต (Feminist Twitter)” → widely perceived as politically active women  
     * “สาววาย / แม่บ้านทวิตเตอร์ / สายมูผู้หญิง”   
       (BL fangirls / housewife Twitter / spiritual-believer women)→ typically associated with women  
     * “แม่เลี้ยงเดี่ยว”, “สาวออฟฟิศ”, “แม่ค้า”, “เมียฝรั่ง”   
       (single mom/office lady/female vendor/foreign man’s wife) → directly imply women  
     * “พ่อบ้านใจกล้า”, “ผู้ชายสายเปย์” , “ผู้ชายแมน ๆ”   
       (the brave househusband / big-spending man / sugar-daddy type / manly man) → tied to masculine roles

3) **Gender or SOGI Coded Slang in Thai Culture**  
   Slang, mockery, or double-meaning jokes that implicitly refer to specific gender or sexual identity groups:  
   * **Gay male–coded sexual slang**, e.g.  
     Eg. “ถั่วดำ”, “อัดถั่วดำ”, “ขุดทอง”, “สายเหลือง”, “ฟ้าเหลือง”, “ล้างตู้เย็น”, “ตุ๋ย”,  “มนต์รักฟักทองบด”   
     (black beans — anal sex reference / pounding black beans — gay sex verb / gold digging — anal sex slang / yellow sky — sex slang / cleaning the fridge — anal prep joke / to fuck / to have sex — specifically referring to gay male sex / mashed pumpkin — gay porn parody title reference)  
     * **Lesbian-coded slang**, e.g.  
       Eg. “ตีฉิ่ง” (playing cymbals” — slang referring to lesbian)

If such terms are used in a stereotyping, degrading, mocking, or negative evaluative way, they are considered to meet **Criterion (A)**.

If a message uses sexual language or profanity targeting only a single individual, e.g., “มึง”, “เขา”, “พี่คนนี้” (you/they/this person) without elevating it to a gender group, it does NOT pass Criterion (A) and must be classified as **NON-GB**.

However, there are some cases to be considered

3. **Inherently Gendered Slur Test**  
   Some slurs are gender-discriminatory by definition. These are considered to meet Criterion (A) even when used against only one person and without generalizing a group.  
   * **Woman-Degrading Slurs**  
     Slurs that directly degrade women through sexual morality, body, or value:   
     “กะหรี่”, “ร่าน”, “โสเภณี”, “สุนัขตัวเมีย”, “หน้าตัวเมีย”, “สำส่อน”, “อีตัว”  
     (whore, slut, prostitute, female dog/bitch, bitch-faced, promiscuous)  
   *  **Anti-LGBT / SOGI Slurs**  
     Slurs that intentionally degrade gender identity or sexual orientation:  
      “อีตุ๊ด”, “ลักเพศ”, “ผิดเพศ”, “พวกเบี่ยงเบียนทางเพศ”, “ถังขี้”, “ประตูหลัง”  
     (faggot, pervert, gender deviant, sexual deviants, toilet slang, backdoor-sex slur)  
   * **Gendered Sexualized Slurs**  
     Sexualized insults attacking gender-based worth through genitals, sexual role, or sexual performance:  
      “จู๋เล็ก”, “ควยกุด”, “ควยไม่มีค่า”, “หอยเน่า”, “ตูดหลวม”, “นมปลอม”, “นมยาน”，“ชอบแหกหี”  
     (small dick, stub dick, worthless dick, rotten pussy, loose ass, fake boobs, saggy boobs, likes spreading pussy)

### **5.2 Criterion (B): Evaluative or Stereotypical Judgment Toward a Gender Group** 

After Criterion (A) is met, content is classified as GB only if it also shows a negative evaluation or discriminatory attitude toward the gender group, through one or more of the following:

1. **Insulting / Derogatory / Dehumanizing**  
    	Attributing negative traits to all or most of a gender group, such as intelligence, emotions, morality, ability, personality, or behavior.  
   **Note:** Insulting one person only, without elevating it to the gender group, does NOT pass Criterion (B).  
2. **Negative Generalization**  
   	Attributing negative traits to all or most of a gender group, such as intelligence, emotions, morality, ability, personality, or behavior.  
   **Note:** The generalization must target a gender group directly. If it targets a non-gender group, it is not GB.  
3. **Justification of Harm**  
   	Statements that suggest a gender group deserves violence, sexual abuse, or rights violations because of their gender or gender expression.  
4. **Value Tied to Gendered Traits**  
   	Judging a group’s human worth or dignity based on body size,  physical traits, genitals, sexual performance, or gendered physical features. This applies to men, women, and LGBTQ+.   
   **Note:** If a single person is insulted using sexual traits, it still passes Criterion (B).  
5. **Positive or Benevolent Stereotype**  
   	A message may also pass Criterion (B) when it praises or describes a gender group in a way that imposes fixed traits, roles, or expectations. Even if the statement appears positive, it can still be biased if it suggests that a gender group is naturally suited to a particular behaviour, personality, responsibility, or social role.  
   **Note**: A statement that merely compliments a specific individual or describes a personal experience does not pass Criterion (B) unless it generalizes the trait to a gender group. 

If a message discusses gender in an informational, factual, descriptive, or complimentary manner, without assigning roles, duties, or fixed characteristics to a gender group, it does not satisfy Criterion (B) and must be classified as **NON-GB**.

### **5.3 Criterion (C): The Message Must NOT Be Meta Commentary / Social Critique / Counter-Speech**

Even if slurs or offensive language appear, the message is **NON-GB** if the speaker is:

1. **Meta commentary**  
   Referring to someone else’s sexism for the purpose of criticizing or condemning it, e.g.:  
   * “ยังมีคนพูดว่าผู้หญิงโง่อยู่เลย น่าสมเพชมาก”  
     (There are still people saying women are stupid — that’s pathetic.)  
2. **Social critique**  
   Criticizing social structures or gender-discriminatory norms, e.g.:  
   * “สังคมไทยชอบโทษผู้หญิงเวลาโดนคุกคามทางเพศ”  
     (Thai society tends to blame women when they are sexually harassed.)  
3. **Counter-speech**  
   Actively opposing gender bias, e.g.:  
   * “ใครบอกว่าผู้หญิงขับรถไม่เก่ง นั่นคืออคติล้วน ๆ”  
     (Whoever says women can’t drive is completely biased.)

Key Test for Criterion (C)

* If the speaker is expressing their own bias → likely GB  
* If the speaker is criticizing or resisting someone else’s bias → classify as NON-GB

### **5.4 Criterion (D): Gendered-Insult (Not GB)**

Some messages contain gender reference \+ insult but are **NOT Gender Bias**, because they **do not pass Criterion (B)**

These cases occur when:

* The insult targets a **single individual**, not a gender group  
* The insult is **NOT an inherently gendered slur**  
* There is no intention to generalize about a gender group  
* Gender is used only as a referential identifier, not the target of bias

These are classified as **NON-GB** but should be **separately tagged as Gendered-Insult (D)** due to their high confusion risk.

**Examples of Gendered-Insult (D)**

* “คนนั้นอะ **ผู้ชายโง่ๆ**”   
  (That guy is a stupid man.)  
* “ผู้หญิงคนนั้น **ขี้วีนมาก**”  
  (That woman is extremely temperamental.): specific individual, not women as a group  
* “ผู้ชายคนนี้แม่ง **น่ารำคาญฉิบ**”  
  (This man is damn annoying.)  
* “เกย์คนนั้น **ชอบเถียง**”   
  (That gay guy argues a lot.): not a slur, not generalizing LGBTQ

**Decision Rules for Labeling Gendered-Insult (D)**

All conditions must be met:

* Gender is referenced (**passes A at referential level**)  
* A negative term is present  
* The attack targets **only one person**  
* The negative term is **not inherently gender-discriminatory**  
* There is **no negative generalization** of the gender group (**fails B**)

**Important Notes**

* Criterion (D) exists to separate **“gender \+ insult”** from **true Gender Bias (GB)**  
* Messages should **NOT be labeled GB** just because they contain  
   “ผู้ชาย / ผู้หญิง / เกย์ / ทอม / ดี้” (man/woman/gay/tom/dee)  
* The intent must be to **attack the gender as a group**, not just identify the person  
* This is distinct from **GENDER\_MENTION (neutral mention)** and from **true GB (A \+ B)**

| Sentence | Span | Label |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| That guy is a stupid man. | Stupid man | **GENDERED\_INSULT** |
| That woman is obnoxiously argumentative. | woman is obnoxiously argumentative. | **GENDERED\_INSULT** |
| This guy is a dumb gay (not attacking gay people, targeting only one person). | dumb gay | **GENDERED\_INSULT** |

### **5.5 Additional Criteria for Messages Containing Sexual / Obscene Language**

To handle ambiguous cases involving references to genitals or sexual language, apply the following rules:

**Classify as NON-GB if the message:**

* Uses sexual or obscene language toward a specific individual only (e.g., “you,” “him,” “this person”)  
* Does not generalize the target to a gender group  
* Does not use the body or genitals as a basis for judging the value, dignity, or gender identity of any group  
* Does not qualify as an inherently gendered slur under Criterion 5.1

In these cases, the message must be classified as **NON-GB**, **even if it constitutes sexual harassment or an obscene insult.**

**Classify as Potential GB if the message:**

* Elevates the target to a gender group, such as:  
  * “ผู้ชายทั้งเพศ” *(all men)*  
  * “ผู้หญิงพวกนี้” *(these women)*  
  * “ตุ๊ดพวกนี้” *(these faggots)*  
  * “คนจู๋เล็กทั้งนั้น” *(all small-dick people)*  
* Uses sex, genitals, or the body to:  
  * Devalue  
  * Shame  
  * Or negatively generalize a gender group

In such cases, the message is considered to **likely pass Criteria (A) and (B)** and **must be further evaluated under Criterion (C)** before final classification as **GB**.

### **5.6 Neutral Messages** 

Messages that:

* Do not contain any gender-related content, either explicitly or implicitly  
* Do not meet Criteria (A), (B), (C), or (D)

Must be classified as **Neutral**.

Examples of Neutral Messages:

* “วันนี้อากาศดีมาก” (The weather is great today.)  
* “กำลังจะไปทำงาน” (About to go to work.)  
* “ง่วงนอนสุด ๆ” (So sleepy.)

These messages **require NO GB or NON-GB label.**

### **5.7 Final Decision Summary**

A message is classified as **Gender Bias (GB)** only when ALL of the following are true:

1. There is a reference to gender / gender identity / sexual orientation as a group   
   → Passes Criterion 5.1 (Gendered Target)  
2. There is negative evaluation, derogation, dehumanization, or devaluation based on gender → Passes Criterion 5.2 (Negative Evaluation)  
3. The message is not Meta commentary/ Social critique/ Counter-speech   
   → Does NOT fall under Criterion 5.3  
4. The message is not a single-person gendered insult used referentially   
   → Does NOT fall under Criterion 5.4 (Gendered-Insult)

A message is classified as **Non-Gender Bias (NON-GB)** when it falls under either of the following:

1. It discusses, criticizes, or counters someone else’s gender bias  
   → Qualifies under Criterion 5.3 (META / COUNTER)  
2. It insults a single individual using gender referentially, without generalizing a gender group, and without using a gendered slur   
   → Qualifies under Criterion 5.4 (GENDERED\_INSULT)

All other messages must be classified as: **Neutral**

## **6\. Trigger and Rationale**

### **6.1 Definitions**

1) **Trigger**  
   The shortest possible text span that contains the core GB element, such as:  
* A gender reference  
* An insult/generalization / sexualized term tied to gender  
2) **Rationale**  
   A slightly longer span from the original sentence that still clearly explains why the content is GB, with the following constraints:  
* Not a rewritten explanation  
* Uses only the original wording  
* Slightly wider than the Trigger (to show reasoning)  
* Still shorter than the full sentence

### **6.2 Rules for Selecting a Trigger (GB Cases)**

* Must be the minimal span  
* Must contain a core GB component, such as a gender reference/ a gender-linked insult/a generalization / sexualized term  
* If the case is GB-SEX, the Trigger must include the sexualized insult  
* If GB-NORMATIVE, include the stereotype or gender-role phrase  
* If GB-ATTACK, include the slur or derogatory phrase  
* Do not include unnecessary surrounding text

**Example:**  “ทำไมพวกผญ ทรงที่เป็นเฟมทวิต ถึงหน้าเหี้ยและ type แบบเดียวกันหมด”  
(Why do these women who look like Feminist Twitter all have the same ugly faces and the same type?)  
**Trigger:** “พวกผญ,เฟมทวิต,เหี้ย” (these women, Feminist Twitter, ugly faces)

### **6.3 Rules for Selecting a Rationale (GB Cases)**

* Must include the key context that confirms GB  
* Must be a span taken directly from the original sentence  
* Should be shorter than the full sentence  
* Must explain GB more clearly than the Trigger alo**ne**  
* Must **not** be rewritten by the annotator

**Example:**  “ทำไมพวกผญ ทรงที่เป็นเฟมทวิต ถึงหน้าเหี้ยและ type แบบเดียวกันหมด”  
(Why do these women who look like Feminist Twitter all have the same ugly faces and the same type?)  
**Rationale:**  

* “พวกผญ ทรงที่เป็นเฟมทวิต ถึงหน้าเหี้ย” (Why do these women who look like Feminist Twitter all have the same ugly faces)  
* “พวกผญ ทรงที่เป็นเฟมทวิต type แบบเดียวกันหมด” (Why do these women who look like Feminist Twitter all have the same type?)

### **6.4 Trigger Selection for Non-GB (Cases C and D)**

For **NON-GB**, the Trigger is used to indicate why the message is NOT classified as GB. The selection rules differ by subtype:

#### **6.4.1 (C) META / COUNTER → Trigger \= the part that shows the speaker is NOT the one expressing bias**

Trigger must be a word or phrase that indicates the speaker is:

* Referring to someone else’s biased statement  
* Critiquing social norms or structures  
* Refuting or countering bias  
  **Select only the part that signals the meta/counter stance**, such as:  
* Reporting verbs  
* Evaluation of bias  
* Condemnation markers  
  **Do NOT select the slur itself**, because the purpose of the Trigger here is not to extract bias terms, but to show: “This is NOT GB because the speaker is not the one expressing the bias.”  
  **Example:** “ยังมีคนพูดว่าผู้หญิงโง่อยู่เลย น่าสมเพชมาก”  
  (There are still people saying women are stupid — that’s pathetic.)  
  **Trigger META:**   
* “ยังมีคนพูดว่า” (There are still people saying)  
* “น่าสมเพชมาก” (that’s pathetic.)  
  **Reason:** These clearly indicate that the speaker is **criticizing others**, not expressing the bias directly.

  #### **6.4.2 (D) GENDERED-INSULT → Trigger \= the span showing a single-person gendered insult**

  **Trigger must contain:**  
  * A gender reference  
  * A negative descriptor  
  * Targeted at a single individual  
  * Use a minimal span  
  * Do not include unnecessary sentence material

  **Do NOT select expansions that make it look like a group-level attack**, because that would incorrectly turn a **Non-GB (D)** case into **GB**.

  **Example:** “คนนั้นอะ ผู้ชายโง่ๆ” (That guy is a stupid man.)

  **Trigger D:** “คนนั้นอะ” (That guy)

### **6.5  Rationale Selection for Non-GB (Cases C and D)**

Rationale for **Non-GB** explains why the content is NOT GB, even though gender-related content appears.

#### **6.5.1 (C) META / COUNTER → Rationale must include full confirmation context**

Rationale must include the parts that show:

* The speaker is criticizing  
* Reporting someone else’s statement  
* Identifying the statement as biased  
* Condemning discrimination  
*  Include only what is necessary to prove it is meta/counter  
  **Example:** “สังคมไทยชอบโทษผู้หญิงเวลาโดนคุกคามทางเพศ”  
  (Thai society tends to blame women when they are sexually harassed.)  
  **Rationale META :** “สังคมไทยชอบโทษผู้หญิงเวลาโดนคุกคามทางเพศ”  
  (Thai society tends to blame women when they are sexually harassed.)

  **Reason:** The entire phrase functions as a social critique.

  #### **6.5.2 (D) GENDERED-INSULT → Rationale must confirm it targets a single individual**

  Rationale must include:

* The part indicating the speaker refers to **one specific person**  
* Gender \+ negative term \+ single-person reference Eg. “คนนั้นอะ+ผู้ชาย+โง่ๆ” (That guy \+ is a stupid \+  man.)  
*  Must **not** summarize in the annotator’s own words  
* Must use **only original spans**

### **6.6 Key Warnings and Common Errors**

Trigger and Rationale for Non-GB must **never accidentally turn into GB**. To avoid misclassification:

#### **6.6.1  Do NOT select slurs alone as META/COUNTER Triggers**

“ยังมีคนพูดว่าผู้หญิงโง่อยู่เลย น่าสมเพชมาก”  
(There are still people saying women are stupid — that’s pathetic.)

**Wrong:** Trigger \= “ผู้หญิง”,“โง่” (women, stupid)  
**Correct:** Trigger \= “คนพูดว่า”, “ยังมีคนคิดว่า”, “น่าสมเพชมาก” (There are still people saying, that’s pathetic.)

#### **6.6.2 For (D) Gendered-Insult**

Trigger and Rationale must clearly indicate a single-person insult, not a group attack.

“คนนั้นอะ ผู้ชายโง่ๆ” (That guy is a stupid man.)

**Wrong:** Trigger \= “ผู้ชาย”, “โง่”(man, stupid)  
**Correct:** Trigger \= “คนนั้นอะ” (That guy)→ clearly shows it is a personal insult

#### **6.6.3 For All Non-GB Cases**

Trigger must show **why it is NOT GB**, **not** why it **would be** GB.

#### **6.6.4 Triggers / Rationales for Non-GB May Contain Slur Words**

But only for these purposes:

* To show they are someone else’s words (META)  
* To show they are being countered (COUNTER)  
* To show they are a single-person insult (D)  
*  Never in a context that shows the speaker personally expressing bias

## **7\. Guide to Annotator Tool**

The **Annotator Tool** is a supporting tool for annotating text in order to identify:

* Trigger spans  
* Rationale spans  
* Label type (GB-ATTACK / GB-NORMATIVE / GB-SEX/NON-GB)  
* Decision Framework classification (A / B / C) based on the complete Gender Bias criteria

Before starting, users must upload a **CSV file** in one of the following formats:

* **Contain token**  
  id, tokens  
  1, \["ผู้","หญิง","มัน","โง่"\]  
  2, \["มึง","มัน","จู๋","เล็ก"\]  
* **Contain text**  
  id, text  
  1, "ผู้หญิงมันโง่"  
  2, "มึงมันจู๋เล็ก"

### **7.1 Annotation Workflow**

1) **Select a Sentence**  
   After uploading the CSV, the tool will display:  
* The first sentence  
* With its tokenized words

	Use the navigation button

* **N** \= Go to next sentence  
* **P** \= Go back to the previous sentence

2) **Creating Rationale and Trigger**  
   The Annotator operates in two modes:  
* **Rationale Mode (R)** → highlighted in pink  
* **Trigger Mode (T)** → highlighted in red  
  Use shortcut keys:  
* Press R to switch to Rationale Mode  
* Press T to switch to Trigger Mode  
  **Selecting a Rationale (Rationale Mode)**  
  Drag to select tokens to create a Rationale span, must include:  
* At least 1 Rationale span  
* Each Rationale must contain at least 1 Trigger span  
  **Selecting a Trigger (Trigger Mode)**  
  Drag to select tokens to create a **Trigger span,** must include:

* At least 1 Trigger span

3) **Selecting the Bias Type for Each Rationale**  
   While in Rationale Mode, select one of the following Bias Types:  
* GB-ATTACK  
* GB-NORMATIVE  
* GB-SEX

4) **Decision Framework**  
   The annotator must select the appropriate Decision Framework category to justify each Rationale and determine whether the message should be classified as GB or NON-GB.  
* **A — Gendered Target**  
  The message contains a gender, gender identity, or SOGI target, either explicitly or implicitly.  
* **B — Negative Evaluation**  
  The message includes negative evaluation toward a gender group, such as: devaluation, discrimination, negative generalization, sexual shaming, or body-based gender insults  
* **C — Meta Commentary**  
  This category is selected when the speaker is critiquing, quoting, or condemning someone else’s gender bias, rather than expressing bias themselves. When **C** is selected, **A and B must not be selected**, the message must be classified as **NON-GB**, and **Trigger and Rationale selection is not required**.  
* **D — Gendered Insult**  
  This category is selected when the speaker is insulting or criticizing a single individual while using gender only as a referential identifier, without expressing discrimination toward a gender group. When **D** is selected, **A and B must not be selected**, the message must be classified as **NON-GB**, and **Trigger and Rationale must be selected in accordance with Section 6.4.2 (Trigger) and Section 6.5.2 (Rationale)**. The content in this category **does not constitute gender-based discrimination**.

5) **Saving a Rationale**  
   Before clicking Finish Rationale, all of the following conditions must be met:  
* At least 1 Rationale span  
* At least 1 Trigger span  
* Rationale ID is assigned (auto-generated)  
* Bias Type is selected  
* At least 1 Decision Framework criterion is selected  
  * Either A \+ B  
  * Or C / D only (but never both)

6) **Validation and Saving**

After completing Trigger and Rationale selection:

* Click **Finish Annotation for This Sentence**  
* Then choose:  
  * **Confirm & Next Sentence** → save and continue  
  * **Back to Annotation** → edit details

7) **Exporting the Annotations**  
   Once all sentences have been annotated, click Download CSV to export the full annotation results
